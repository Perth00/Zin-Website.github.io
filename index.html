About Me

Passion Meets Excellence

Hi, I'm Zin, a dedicated Taekwondo Poomsae coach and former Malaysian national team athlete. I started my Taekwondo journey at age 4, and after 16 years of training and competing, I've reached the World Top 20+ ranking, competed in World Championships, and brought home gold from the SEA Games. Now at 20, I've retired from competition to focus on coaching the next generation.

My coaching philosophy centers on building strong fundamentals, developing precise technique, and cultivating the mental discipline that separates good practitioners from great ones.

  • Personalized training programs for all skill levels
  • Competition preparation and mental coaching
  • Video analysis and technique refinement
  • Flexible scheduling for students and professionals

Not at all! I welcome complete beginners. My Beginner program focuses on building strong fundamentals from scratch. If you already have experience, we'll assess your current level and work on advancing your skills.

Poomsae (also spelled Poomse) is the "forms" or "patterns" aspect of Taekwondo. It involves performing a sequence of defensive and offensive movements against imaginary opponents. It emphasizes precision, balance, breathing, and technique—like martial arts choreography judged on form and execution.

Beginner sessions are 1 hour, Intermediate sessions are 1.5 hours, and Competition Prep sessions are 2 hours. This ensures enough time for warm-up, technique practice, drills, and cool-down appropriate for each level.

For your first few sessions, comfortable athletic wear (t-shirt and shorts/leggings) is fine. Bring water and a towel. If you decide to continue, I recommend getting a dobok (Taekwondo uniform). Training is done barefoot.

I primarily offer private 1-on-1 coaching for personalized attention. However, I also do small group sessions (2-4 people) at a discounted rate—great for friends or family who want to train together. Contact me for group pricing!
